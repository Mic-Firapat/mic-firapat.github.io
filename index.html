Ian Riviere

Master's Degree Possessor, Card Game Umpire, Gaming Enthusiast and Problems' foe.

It is fairly common for human's engines to use caffeine (also known as theine) as their main fuel. This is not the case for Ian Riviere's one, which has been powered by curiosity since he was able to formulate an intelligible question1which was before he could understand most answers.. A strong desire to understand the world around him and the causes behind events (be it fortunate ones or less desirable ones) pushed him to pursue scientific studies.

Once one is able to grasp a better understanding of undesirable occurences and their sources, it is but natural to wish to save the day and rid the world of those. However, it is definitely funnier to solve problems caused by one's own incompetence with a keyboard and a plethora of transistors instead of tackling some real problems like world hunger, pineapple on a pizza or global warming. That is the reason why Ian spent great amounts of time fixing Segmentation Faults and Syntax Errors, acting as a computer wizard in front of friends thanks to a renowned penguin and reading the remarkably precise error messages produced by MariaDB or MySQL's parsers while thanking GNU Bison for it.

Now that Ian Rivere is finally endowed with public aknowledgement of his academical abilities in the form of a piece of paper named a "Master's Degree"2 in Data and Connected Systems and somewhat capable of dealing with previously mentioned problems, he is eager to help companies by bringing in his natural curiosity and cheerfulness as well as his skills wrought during his academic curriculum, experiences in R&D and personal explorations. Do not be afraid of contacting him, it will be his pleasure to answer you swiftly and to take opportunities of tackling new problems and discovering new amazing technologies.

You might also stumble accros Ian in collectible card game tournaments as a certified Judge, where he willingly goes through the trouble of helping others have fun in a fair and competitive environment while he himself is not allowed to play.

Automatic Music Quiz Generator

Ian and his friends have fun playing music quizzes available on YouTube. Unfortunately, it is hard to find videos covering exactly the kind of content that this group of scoundrels affectionates. Hence, Ian has developed a small project, which is not the most attractive in form but whose content is functional.

The audio files for the musics is downloaded downloaded automatically, as well as the image representating the game (which is retrieved thanks to RAWG's API). The video itself is composed of clips generated by a Pyhon Script, witch a normalised audio track.

Note that the video probably has to be seen on YouTube, considering a lot of tracks are copyrighted, even though this is fair use.
